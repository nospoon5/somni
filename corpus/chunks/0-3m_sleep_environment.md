# Creating a Soothing Sleep Environment for Your Newborn (0-3 Months)

Setting up a comfortable and safe sleep environment is key for your newborn, helping them feel secure and encouraging healthy sleep patterns. In these early months, babies are still adjusting to the world outside the womb and learning the difference between day and night. Your thoughtful approach to light, noise, and clothing can make a big difference.

## Key Points
- Newborns are developing their day-night rhythm, so light cues are important.
- A consistent, calm soundscape can help your baby settle.
- Dressing your baby appropriately and safely is crucial for comfort and safety.

## What to Try
- **Light:** Help your baby distinguish day from night by exposing them to natural bright light during the day and keeping their sleep space dim or dark at night. When you need to attend to your baby overnight, use a soft, dim light to minimise stimulation.
- **Noise:** Aim for a quiet and calm sleep environment. Some babies find consistent, low-level background noise, like white noise or soft music, very soothing. If using a sound machine, place it well away from your baby's ears and keep the volume low to protect their hearing. Sudden noises can easily startle a newborn, so a consistent sound can help mask these.
- **Clothing:** Dress your baby in layers of fitted clothing, similar to how you'd dress yourself to be comfortably warm. It's vital to keep your baby's head uncovered – avoid hats, beanies, or hoods during sleep, as babies regulate their temperature through their head and face. Wrapping or swaddling can be very effective for calming newborns and reducing their startle reflex. Use lightweight, breathable fabrics like cotton or muslin, ensuring the wrap is firm but allows for hip movement and keeps their arms above waist level, with their face and head completely uncovered. A safe infant sleeping bag with fitted neck and armholes and no hood is also an excellent option from birth. Remember to discontinue wrapping at the first signs of your baby starting to roll, which is typically around 3-4 months.

## When to Seek Help
- If you're concerned your baby is consistently too hot or too cold, or if they seem distressed by their sleep environment despite your efforts, speak to your child and family health nurse or GP.
- If your baby is very sleepy or hard to wake, especially if they are premature or have health concerns, always consult your doctor or child health nurse.
