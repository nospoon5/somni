# Creating a Safe and Soothing Sleep Environment for Your Newborn (0-3 Months)

Creating a comfortable and safe sleep environment is key to supporting your newborn's rest and development. For babies aged 0-3 months, their sleep patterns are still developing, and they rely on you to help them feel secure and ready for sleep. Focusing on appropriate light, consistent noise, and safe clothing can make a big difference.

Newborns are learning the difference between day and night, so your environment can help guide their internal clock. They also have delicate airways and are not yet able to move themselves out of unsafe positions, making safe clothing and a clear sleep space vital.

## Key Points
- **Light and Dark:** Help your baby distinguish day from night by exposing them to natural light during the day and keeping things dim or dark at night.
- **Consistent Noise:** While some babies prefer quiet, others find gentle, consistent background noise soothing.
- **Safe Clothing:** Dress your baby in layers, ensure their head is uncovered, and use wrapping or a safe sleeping bag appropriately for their age and developmental stage.

## What to Try
- **Manage Light:** During the day, allow natural light into your baby's sleep space. At night, keep the room dark or use a very dim night light for feeds and changes. This helps their circadian rhythm develop. There's no evidence that red light specifically aids baby sleep.
- **Control Noise:** Aim for a consistent noise level. If your home is usually quiet, try to maintain that. If it's naturally a bit noisy, soft music or white noise (like a fan or a dedicated app) can help mask sudden sounds. Always keep the volume low and the device away from your baby's ears to protect their hearing.
- **Dress for Comfort and Safety:**
    - **Layers:** Dress your baby in layers of fitted clothing, similar to how you'd dress yourself for the room temperature. You can add or remove layers as needed.
    - **Head Uncovered:** Always ensure your baby's head and face are uncovered during sleep. Hats or beanies can cause overheating and may slip to cover their face, posing a suffocation risk.
    - **Check Temperature:** Feel your baby's tummy or back to check their temperature; they should feel warm, not hot or sweaty. Their hands and feet might feel cool, which is normal.
    - **Wrapping/Swaddling:** For newborns, wrapping in lightweight cotton or muslin can help them feel secure and reduce the startle reflex. Ensure the wrap is firm but allows hip movement and chest expansion, doesn't go above the shoulders, and leaves their head uncovered. Stop wrapping as soon as your baby shows signs of starting to roll (typically around 3-4 months).
    - **Sleeping Bags:** A safe infant sleeping bag or suit (with fitted neck and armholes, and no hood) is a great alternative to blankets and can be used from birth.
    - **Blankets:** If using blankets, choose thin, lightweight ones, tuck them in firmly at chest level, and position your baby's feet at the bottom of the cot to prevent them from wiggling up and covering their face.

## When to Seek Help
- If you're concerned your baby is consistently too hot or too cold, or if they seem unwell, always consult your doctor or child health nurse.
- If your baby is very sleepy or hard to wake, especially if they are not feeding enough or gaining weight, seek advice from a health professional.
