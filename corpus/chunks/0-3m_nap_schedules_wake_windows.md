# Nap Schedules and Wake Windows for 0-3 Month Olds

Welcome to the world of newborn sleep, where patterns are unique and constantly evolving! In these early months, your baby is learning about day and night, and their sleep is often spread across the entire 24-hour period. It's a time for flexibility and responding to your little one's cues.

Newborns typically sleep between 14 to 17 hours over a 24-hour cycle, usually in short bursts of 2 to 4 hours at a time. Their sleep cycles are quite short, around 40-50 minutes, comprising both active and quiet sleep. It's common for babies to stir or fully wake at the end of a sleep cycle and need a little help to settle back down.

As your baby approaches 6 to 12 weeks, you might start to notice slightly longer stretches of sleep at night, possibly up to 4-5 hours, as their day-night rhythm begins to emerge. However, frequent night feeds are still very normal and expected.

## Key Points
- **Total Sleep:** 14-17 hours per 24 hours, spread across day and night.
- **Nap Duration:** Typically 2-4 hours per sleep period, but can be as short as 20-40 minutes.
- **Wake Windows (0-6 weeks):** Around 1 to 2 hours.
- **Wake Windows (6-12 weeks):** Around 1 to 2.5 hours.
- **Night Sleep:** Frequent waking for feeds is normal; longer stretches (4-5 hours) may start to appear by 3 months.
- **Flexibility is Key:** Newborns don't follow strict schedules; respond to their hunger and tired cues.

## What to Try
- **Observe Tired Signs:** Look for cues like yawning, frowning, grizzling, clenching fists, or jerky movements. Responding promptly helps prevent your baby from becoming overtired, which can make settling harder.
- **Follow a Flexible Feed, Play, Sleep Pattern:** Offer a feed when your baby wakes, engage in gentle play (10-20 minutes is often enough for newborns), change their nappy, then put them down for sleep when tired signs appear.
- **Emphasise Day and Night Differences:** Keep daytime bright and active, and nighttime dim and quiet. Use soft voices and minimal interaction during night feeds to help establish their circadian rhythm.
- **Gentle Settling:** Newborns often need help to settle. Try holding, gentle rhythmic patting, rocking, or soft singing. Placing them down drowsy but awake can help them learn to associate their cot with falling asleep.
- **Safe Sleep Environment:** Always place your baby on their back on a firm, flat, level surface in a clear cot or bassinet. Keep their head and face uncovered. Room-sharing (baby in their own safe sleep space in your room) is recommended for at least the first 6 months.

## When to Seek Help
- If your baby is very sleepy or hard to wake, especially if they're not feeding enough or gaining weight as expected, consult your doctor or child health nurse.
- If you're concerned about your baby's overall behaviour or if they are crying excessively and you can't determine the cause, reach out to a health professional.
- If you are feeling overwhelmed or struggling with broken sleep, remember support is available from parent helplines or early parenting centres.
