# Independent Settling: Common Mistakes

When a parent says "sleep is bad" or "we are doing everything and it is still awful", the problem is often not effort. It is usually one of a small number of pattern errors that keep resetting the learning.

## The Big Four Mistakes

### 1. Changing the plan every night

Night one is patting. Night two is rocking. Night three is feeding back to sleep. Night four is timed check-ins. That does not create flexibility. It creates confusion.

### 2. Fixing sleep associations and schedule at the same time without a plan

If the baby is overtired **and** being asked to settle in a new way, the parent often blames the method when the real issue is that the starting conditions were poor.

### 3. Giving lots of help after bedtime but expecting independent resettling overnight

If the baby falls asleep fully rocked or fed at 7:30 pm, it is unsurprising that they ask for the same setup at 1:30 am.

### 4. Responding too much or too little

Some babies escalate if the parent keeps popping in and out. Others panic if the parent disappears too fast. The method has to fit the baby's response, not the internet script.

## Quick Triage For A Vague Query

If the parent gives almost no detail, start by checking these in order:

1. Is the schedule age-appropriate?
2. How does the baby fall asleep at bedtime?
3. What happens at the first wake?
4. Is the parent using one response or five?

## What To Do Tonight

- **Pick one target.** Usually bedtime first.
- **Pick one response.** Brief check-ins, hands-on settling, or camping out.
- **Use the same response for the first non-feed wake.**
- **Keep the room dark and the interaction flat.**
- **Judge the plan over 3 nights, then 7.**

## Reset Plan If Everything Feels Messy

- go back to an age-appropriate bedtime
- keep the routine short and predictable
- end feeds before the final sleep step if feeds are part of the problem
- put down awake or at least less assisted than usual
- hold the line with one settling response

## What To Stop Doing

- do not add a feed at a brand-new wake just because tonight is hard
- do not rescue every protest immediately if the chosen plan includes a brief pause
- do not keep the baby up longer and longer hoping they will crash harder
- do not expect naps to fix first if bedtime is still inconsistent

## When To Seek Help

- the baby sounds in pain, unwell, or medically uncomfortable
- the parent cannot stay calm enough to apply the plan safely
- the same consistent plan is being used and there is still no progress after 1-2 weeks
