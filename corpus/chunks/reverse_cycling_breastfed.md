# Reverse Cycling in a Breastfed Baby

Reverse cycling is when a breastfed baby takes less milk during the day and then makes up for it with more frequent feeds overnight. This often shows up when babies are more distracted in the daytime, start daycare, spend part of the day away from the breastfeeding parent, or become very efficient feeders and prefer to feed when everything is quiet.

If your baby is having plenty of wet nappies and is gaining weight well, reverse cycling is usually more exhausting than dangerous. It does not automatically mean your supply is low or that something is wrong. Some babies simply shift more of their intake to the night for a while.

## Key Points
- Reverse cycling is common in breastfed babies, especially during changes like daycare, return to work, or busier daytime routines.
- Good weight gain and normal wet nappies are reassuring signs that milk intake is still adequate overall.
- Feeding more at night does not always mean your baby needs formula or solids.
- The goal is usually to gently pull more feeds back into the day, not to force night weaning suddenly.

## What to Try
- **Offer more daytime feeds:** Feed a little more often during the day, even if your baby does not ask clearly. Quiet, low-distraction feeds can help.
- **Use the drowsy windows:** Try a feed just after waking, before naps, or right before sleep when your baby is calmer and less distracted.
- **Keep nights dark and boring:** At night, use dim light, very little talking, and no play so your baby does not get the message that nighttime is social time.
- **Protect breast access when together:** If you are separated during the day, offer the breast freely when reunited instead of trying to stretch feeds out.
- **Check bottle-feeding support if relevant:** If another carer is offering expressed milk, paced bottle-feeding and regular daytime offers can help your baby take more milk before night.

## When to Seek Help
- Get feeding support if weight gain is slowing, wet nappies drop off, your baby seems unusually sleepy for feeds, or you are worried about supply.
- If overnight feeding has suddenly increased alongside illness, pain, or a major sleep change, look at those causes too rather than assuming it is only reverse cycling.
