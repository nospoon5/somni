# Supporting Your Older Baby's Sleep Environment (7-12 Months)

As your baby approaches their first birthday, they are becoming increasingly mobile and aware of their surroundings. Their sleep patterns are more established, but developmental milestones like crawling or separation anxiety can sometimes affect sleep. Maintaining a consistent and safe sleep environment is crucial to support their evolving needs.

## Key Points
- Consistent light and noise cues help reinforce established sleep patterns.
- Safe clothing choices are vital as your baby becomes more mobile.
- Ensure the sleep space is clear of hazards that a mobile baby might interact with.

## What to Try
- **Light:** Continue to provide a dim or dark environment for all sleep periods, both day and night. This helps signal to your baby that it's time to rest. During waking hours, ensure they get plenty of natural light. If using a night-light, keep it dim and use it only when necessary for comfort or checks, avoiding overstimulation.
- **Noise:** A quiet and consistent sleep environment is generally best. If your baby is accustomed to white noise or soft music, continue to use it at a low volume, ensuring the device is placed safely away from their cot and ears. This can help create a predictable and calming atmosphere, especially if they are more aware of household sounds.
- **Clothing:** At this age, wrapping or swaddling is no longer recommended as your baby is highly mobile and needs their arms and legs free to move safely. A safe infant sleeping bag with fitted neck and armholes and no hood is an excellent choice, keeping them warm without loose bedding. If using blankets, ensure they are lightweight, firmly tucked in at chest level, and your baby's feet are positioned at the bottom of the cot. Dress your baby in layers, similar to your own comfort, and always keep their head uncovered to prevent overheating. If your baby starts pulling themselves up to stand (around 10-11 months), monitor if the sleeping bag restricts their movement or poses a risk, and consider transitioning away from it if needed. Ensure the cot is clear of any items like toys, pillows, or cords that a mobile baby could reach and interact with, as these can pose suffocation or strangulation risks.

## When to Seek Help
- If your baby's sleep patterns suddenly change significantly, or if they are experiencing persistent sleep difficulties, consult your child and family health nurse or GP.
- If you notice any signs of illness, difficulty breathing, or unusual behaviour during sleep, seek medical advice immediately.
