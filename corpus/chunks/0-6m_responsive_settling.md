# Responsive Settling (0-6 Months)

Responsive settling involves soothing your distressed baby in their cot without picking them up immediately, helping them gradually learn to self-settle. 

For young babies, physical contact and rhythmic motions directly act on their nervous system, telling them they are safe, protected, and loved. This builds a foundation of secure attachment which is essential for longer, better sleep in the long run.

## Key Points
- **The Steps to Settling:** Begin by standing by the cot, talking softly. If crying increases, use gentle patting or shushing. If very distressed, rock them, and lay them back down before they completely fall asleep.
- **The "Stepped" Approach:** Start with the least amount of intervention (voice), step up to physical touch (patting), step up to holding (rocking), and then step back down as they calm.
- **Patience is Key:** Young babies do not form "bad habits" when you respond to their needs. You cannot spoil a newborn by holding them for sleep, and there's no rush to enforce strict routines.

## What to Try
- **Hands-on Settling (Patting/Shushing):** Place one hand gently on your baby's chest or back, and use the other to pat firmly but gently in a slow, rhythmic heartbeat pattern. Pair this with a low, continuous "shhh, shhh" sound.
- **The "Heavy Hand" retreat:** As they begin to calm down under your hands, slowly lessen the pressure of your patting, then stop, leaving just a heavy, still, warm hand on them until they drift fully off.
- **When they escalate:** If your baby stays extremely distressed after a few minutes of patting, pick them up until calm. The goal is a relaxed baby, not a screaming one.

## When to Seek Help
- Responsive settling can take up to 20 or 30 minutes in the beginning. If the process is causing intense anxiety or anger, step away safely or ask a partner to take over. If settling takes hours every time, please seek help from your child health nurse.
