# Pulling To Stand Is Wrecking Sleep

This is one of the most common 6-12 month sleep disruptions: the baby learns to pull to stand, practises it all day, then stands in the cot crying because they are tired but cannot get themselves back down smoothly.

This is not usually a schedule disaster and it is not a sign they have forgotten how to sleep. It is a temporary motor-skill problem layered on top of normal sleep.

## What Is Usually Happening

- the baby is waking between cycles, standing automatically, then getting stuck
- they are practising the new skill in the cot because it is the easiest rail to grab
- the day has been more stimulating, so they are also a bit more wired and tired

## What To Do Tonight

- **Help them lie back down calmly and repeatedly.** Keep it boring. Lower them, phrase it simply, leave.
- **Do not turn every stand-up into a long social rescue.** Too much interaction can make the skill practice even more rewarding overnight.
- **Use a slightly earlier bedtime for a few days.** New skills often increase overtiredness even if the real trigger is developmental.

## What To Do Tomorrow

- practise "down" in the daytime, not just "up"
- give plenty of floor time so the cot is not the main practice zone
- if they keep pulling up after naps, practise sitting from standing there too

## The Decision Tree

### If they stand and cry but settle once lowered

This is classic skill disruption. Stay consistent and it usually eases as the skill becomes less novel.

### If they are also refusing naps and bedtime is blowing out

Now there may be a schedule issue layered on top. Use the skill plan **and** protect sleep pressure with an earlier bedtime or a cleaner nap day.

### If they are climbing out

That is no longer just a developmental sleep wobble. It becomes a sleep safety issue and the cot setup needs review.

## What To Stop Doing

- Do not assume every skill wake needs a feed
- Do not fully rock or feed to sleep every time just because the skill phase is annoying
- Do not practise only standing and forget to teach the way back down

## When To Seek Help

- the child seems in pain rather than frustrated
- sleep remains severely disrupted well after the new skill is mastered
- the child is climbing out or the sleep setup no longer feels safe
