# Creating a Safe and Soothing Sleep Environment for Your Baby (4-12 Months)

As your baby grows from 4 to 12 months, their sleep patterns become more established, and they start to interact more with their environment. Maintaining a safe and consistent sleep space, with attention to light, noise, and appropriate clothing, continues to be important for their healthy development and restful sleep.

During this period, babies become more mobile, learning to roll, sit, and even pull to stand. This increased mobility means their sleep environment needs to adapt to ensure their safety, especially regarding bedding and clothing.

## Key Points
- **Light and Dark:** Continue to reinforce the difference between day and night with light exposure.
- **Consistent Noise:** A calm and consistent soundscape supports settling and uninterrupted sleep.
- **Safe Clothing:** Dress your baby in layers, ensure their head is uncovered, and use a safe sleeping bag, especially once they can roll.

## What to Try
- **Manage Light:** Keep the room dimly lit or dark at night to signal sleep time. Use a very soft night light if needed for comfort or night-time care, but avoid bright artificial lights. During the day, expose your baby to natural light to help regulate their body clock. Blocking out morning brightness can help extend sleep.
- **Control Noise:** Aim for a quiet and consistent sleep environment. Sudden noises can easily disturb a baby's sleep. If your home has unavoidable background noise, consider using soft music or white noise at a low volume, placed away from your baby's ears. Consistency in noise levels is more important than absolute silence.
- **Dress for Comfort and Safety:**
    - **Layers:** Dress your baby in layers of fitted clothing, adjusting based on the room temperature. A good guide is to dress them in one more layer than you would wear yourself.
    - **Head Uncovered:** It's crucial that your baby's head and face remain uncovered during sleep. Hats, beanies, or hoods can lead to overheating and pose a suffocation risk if they slip.
    - **Check Temperature:** Regularly check your baby's tummy or back to ensure they are comfortably warm, not hot or sweaty. Cool hands and feet are usually normal.
    - **No Wrapping:** Once your baby shows signs of rolling (typically around 3-4 months), discontinue wrapping or swaddling. Babies need their arms free to push up and lift their head if they roll onto their tummy.
    - **Safe Sleeping Bags/Suits:** A safe infant sleeping bag or suit (with fitted neck and armholes, and no hood) is an excellent choice for this age. It keeps your baby warm without the need for loose blankets, which can be a suffocation hazard as they become more mobile. Some sleeping bags have a TOG (thermal overall grade) rating to help you choose based on room temperature, but always use it as a guide and observe your baby.
    - **Blankets:** If using blankets, ensure they are lightweight, firmly tucked in, and only reach your baby's chest, with their feet positioned at the bottom of the cot. This prevents them from pulling blankets over their face.

## When to Seek Help
- If you're concerned your baby is consistently too hot or too cold, or if they seem unwell, always consult your doctor or child health nurse.
- If your baby's sleep patterns suddenly change significantly, or if you're struggling to manage their sleep, reach out to your child and family health nurse or GP for personalised advice.
