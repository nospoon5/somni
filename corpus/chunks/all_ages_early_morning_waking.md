# Early Morning Waking (Before 6 am)

Early morning waking, especially before 6 am, can be a common and challenging sleep concern for many families. It's natural to hope for longer stretches of sleep, but sometimes our little ones start their day very early. While often associated with toddlers, the principles for encouraging later wake-ups can apply to older babies too.

It's important to understand that simply putting your child to bed later in the evening usually doesn't result in them waking later in the morning. In fact, it can often lead to them becoming overtired and grumpier. As children mature, their sleep patterns naturally evolve, and early waking often improves as daytime naps gradually decrease. Encouraging self-settling skills can also play a significant role in helping them connect sleep cycles in the early hours.

Environmental factors like light and noise can greatly influence early waking. The early morning hours are also when our body temperature is naturally at its lowest, so ensuring your child is comfortably warm can be beneficial. Consistency in your approach to settling and routines is key to helping your child adjust to new sleep patterns.

## Key Points
- Early morning waking (before 6 am) is a common concern, particularly for toddlers, but can affect older babies too.
- Putting your child to bed later doesn't typically lead to later wake-ups; it can result in an overtired and grumpy child.
- This pattern often improves with age, especially as daytime naps naturally shorten or cease.
- Environmental factors such as light, noise, and room temperature significantly influence early waking.
- Developing self-settling skills is crucial for your child to resettle themselves during lighter sleep cycles in the early morning.

## What to Try
- **Optimise the sleep environment:** Ensure your child's room is as dark as possible by using blackout blinds or curtains to block out any morning light. Muffle external noises by closing windows and doors, or consider using a white noise machine at a low, safe volume, placed away from your child's ears.
- **Check room temperature:** Early mornings can be cooler. Dress your child in comfortable layers or use a safe infant sleeping bag to ensure they are comfortably warm, but not too hot, as their body temperature naturally dips.
- **Encourage self-settling:** If your child stirs or grizzles before 6 am, give them a few minutes to see if they can resettle themselves. Responding too quickly might prevent them from learning to fall back asleep independently.
- **Maintain a consistent morning routine:** Avoid turning on bright lights or offering breakfast before 6 am. Keeping the environment dim and quiet until a consistent, desired wake-up time (e.g., 6 am) can help reinforce that it's still sleep time. Delaying breakfast until after 6 am can help prevent early rising.
- **Review overall sleep habits:** Ensure your child has an age-appropriate sleep schedule, including consistent bedtimes and wake times, and that they are generally getting enough sleep. Sometimes, early waking is a sign of an underlying sleep habit that needs adjusting.

## When to Seek Help
- If early morning waking is accompanied by significant distress that doesn't improve with consistent responsive settling.
- If your child consistently seems tired or grumpy during the day despite what appears to be adequate sleep duration.
- If you suspect a medical condition, such as obstructive sleep apnoea (e.g., persistent snoring, gasping), or other health concerns are affecting their sleep.
- If you are feeling overwhelmed, stressed, or if the sleep challenges are significantly impacting your mental health and wellbeing. Your GP or child and family health nurse can offer support and guidance.
