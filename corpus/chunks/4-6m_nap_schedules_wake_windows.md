# Nap Schedules and Wake Windows for 4-6 Month Olds

As your baby moves into the 4-6 month age range, their sleep patterns often become a little more predictable, though changes are still very much a part of the journey. This period is a significant developmental stage, sometimes marked by the "4-month sleep regression," where sleep might temporarily become more unsettled. It's a great time to gently encourage self-settling skills while continuing to respond to your baby's needs.

Babies at this age typically sleep around 14-15 hours in a 24-hour period. You'll likely see a shift towards more consolidated night sleep, with some babies achieving a longer stretch of about 6 hours by 6 months, though waking at least once a night is still common. Daytime sleep usually settles into 2-3 naps, each lasting up to 2 hours.

Your baby's circadian rhythm is maturing, making the distinction between day and night clearer. Observing their tired signs and wake windows is crucial to help them settle more easily and avoid overtiredness.

## Key Points
- **Total Sleep:** 12-15 hours per 24 hours.
- **Nap Duration:** 2-3 daytime naps, each up to 2 hours. Some shorter naps (30-45 minutes) may still occur. Sleep cycles are around 30-50 minutes, and babies may wake after a shorter nap.
- **Wake Windows:** Approximately 1.5 to 2.5 hours.
- **Night Sleep:** Longer stretches of 5-6 hours are possible by 6 months, but waking once or more is still normal.
- **Circadian Rhythm:** Day-night patterns become more established.

## What to Try
- **Responsive Settling:** Continue to respond to your baby's cues for comfort. This might involve gentle patting, rocking, or verbal reassurance. Gradually reducing the intensity of your settling help can encourage self-settling.
- **Consistent Bedtime Routine:** A calming routine before bed, such as a bath, gentle massage, stories, and cuddles, helps signal that it's time for sleep. Aim for a consistent bedtime, ideally between 6:30 pm and 7:30 pm, as babies often experience their deepest sleep between 8 pm and midnight.
- **Drowsy but Awake:** Place your baby in their cot drowsy but still awake. This helps them learn to fall asleep independently and resettle themselves if they wake during the night.
- **Observe Wake Windows:** Pay attention to your baby's tired signs within their typical wake windows (1.5-2.5 hours). Putting them down before they become overtired can make settling much smoother.
- **Dream Feed (Optional):** Some parents find a "dream feed" (a late-night top-up feed between 10 pm and midnight) can help babies sleep longer towards morning.
- **Safe Sleep Environment:** Always ensure your baby sleeps on their back in a clear, firm, flat cot or bassinet. Keep their head and face uncovered. Room-sharing is recommended for at least the first 6 months. Discontinue wrapping when your baby shows signs of rolling.

## When to Seek Help
- If your baby's sleep patterns are causing significant stress or concern for your family, or if you suspect a medical reason for sleep difficulties, consult your doctor or child health nurse.
- If you're struggling with the 4-month sleep regression and feel overwhelmed, support is available from parent helplines or early parenting centres.
- If your baby is not gaining weight well, discuss night feeds with your health professional before attempting to reduce them.
