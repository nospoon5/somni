# Baby Nap Schedules and Wake Windows (4-6 Months)

As your baby moves into the 4-6 month age band, you might start to notice their sleep patterns becoming a little more predictable. This is a lovely stage where their day-night rhythm is more established, and they're developing longer stretches of sleep.

Babies at this age typically need around 12 to 15 hours of sleep in a 24-hour period. Daytime sleep often consolidates into 2 to 3 naps, each lasting up to 2 hours. By 6 months, some babies might even manage a longer stretch of 6 hours overnight, though it's still very common for them to wake at least once for a feed or comfort. Their sleep cycles are also getting longer, becoming more like an adult's, which can mean less frequent waking.

Wake windows, the periods your baby is awake and engaged between naps, are generally extending. For 3-4 month olds, these might be around 1.5 to 2 hours, and for 5-6 month olds, they can stretch to 2 to 2.5 hours. Paying close attention to your baby's tired signs remains crucial to ensure they don't become overtired, which can make settling more challenging.

## Key Points
- Babies aged 4-6 months typically sleep 12-15 hours in 24 hours.
- Daytime sleep usually consists of 2-3 naps, each up to 2 hours.
- Night-time sleep can include stretches of 4-6 hours, but waking once or more is normal.
- Wake windows are generally 1.5-2 hours (3-4 months) and 2-2.5 hours (5-6 months).
- Sleep cycles are lengthening, leading to potentially less frequent night waking.

## What to Try
- **Establish a consistent bedtime routine:** A calming sequence like a bath, gentle massage, pyjamas, quiet stories or songs, and cuddles can signal to your baby that it's time for sleep.
- **Aim for "drowsy but awake":** When your baby shows tired signs, try placing them in their cot when they're drowsy but still awake. This encourages them to learn to fall asleep independently and resettle themselves if they wake.
- **Responsive settling:** If your baby fusses or cries, respond with gentle comfort. This could be hands-on settling (patting, stroking), verbal reassurance, or picking them up for a cuddle until they calm down before returning them to their cot.
- **Discontinue wrapping:** If you've been wrapping your baby, it's time to stop as soon as they show signs of starting to roll (typically around 3-4 months). They need their arms free to push up and move safely.
- **Optimise the sleep environment:** Keep the room dim and quiet during sleep times, both day and night, to support their developing circadian rhythm.

## When to Seek Help
- If your baby seems consistently unhappy or excessively tired during their wakeful periods.
- If you're finding it difficult to manage your baby's sleep or your own sleep needs.
- If you have any concerns about your baby's development or health. Your child health nurse or GP can provide tailored advice and support.
