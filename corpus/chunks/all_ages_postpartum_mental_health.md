# Parental Crisis and Postpartum Mental Health

If you are feeling overwhelmed, at the end of your rope, or like you might hurt yourself or your baby — **please stop reading this and call for help right now.**

- **Lifeline (24/7):** 13 11 14
- **PANDA Helpline (Mon–Sat, 9am–7:30pm AEST):** 1300 726 306
- **Beyond Blue (24/7):** 1300 22 4636
- **Emergency:** 000

If your baby is safe but you are not okay, you are allowed to put the baby down in their cot (on their back, on a firm flat surface, face uncovered) and step away. Leaving a baby to cry for a few minutes in a safe sleep space while you collect yourself is not harmful. Your wellbeing matters too.

## What Is Happening

Postpartum depression and anxiety are not weakness — they are common, recognised medical conditions that affect 1 in 5 mothers and a significant number of fathers and partners. Extreme sleep deprivation can amplify these feelings enormously. If you are having thoughts of harming yourself or your baby, that is a crisis — not a character flaw — and it requires immediate support.

## Key Points

- Intrusive thoughts (including fear of harming your baby) are a recognised symptom of postnatal anxiety and PND. They are distressing precisely because you do NOT want to act on them.
- Feeling rage, despair, or numbness after weeks of broken sleep is not the same as being a bad parent.
- Your baby needs you healthy more than they need any particular sleep method right now.
- You are not alone — PANDA receives thousands of calls every year from parents who feel exactly as you do right now.

## What to Do Right Now

- **If you feel like you might hurt your baby:** Put them down safely in their cot, close the door, and call 000 or PANDA immediately.
- **If you feel like you might hurt yourself:** Call Lifeline (13 11 14) or go to your nearest emergency department.
- **If you are exhausted but safe:** Call PANDA (1300 726 306) or your GP tomorrow morning. Describe exactly what you have told me. Ask for a mental health plan.
- **Longer term:** Ask your GP for a referral to a psychologist who specialises in perinatal mental health. Tresillian and Karitane also offer residential stays for families in crisis — you do not have to keep going alone.

## A Note on Sleep Training

Sleep coaching should be **paused** while you or a primary caregiver are in mental health crisis. Your ability to respond consistently and calmly is the foundation of any sleep approach. When you are ready, I am here to help you build a sustainable plan — but not before you are safe.

## When to Seek Help

- Any thoughts of harming yourself or your baby → call 000 immediately
- Persistent low mood, inability to feel joy, or rage lasting more than two weeks → speak to your GP
- Feeling disconnected from your baby → this is a symptom, not a judgement — mention it to your GP or midwife
