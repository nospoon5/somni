# Sleep Disruption After Vaccination

It is common for babies to sleep differently for a short time after vaccinations. Some are sleepier than usual, while others are more unsettled, wake more often, or struggle with naps because of a sore injection site, a mild fever, or just feeling a bit off.

This kind of disruption is usually temporary and often settles within a couple of days. In this window, the priority is comfort and recovery, not trying to push sleep training or make major plan changes.

## Key Points
- Mild vaccine side effects are usually temporary and often happen in the first few days after vaccination.
- Extra waking, fussiness, tiredness, or nap disruption can all happen for a short period.
- It is usually better to support sleep gently during this window rather than try to teach a new settling skill.

## What to Try
- **Focus on comfort first:** Offer cuddles, feeds, contact, and a calm day. A slightly messier day or night is okay for now.
- **Use pain relief if needed:** If your baby seems uncomfortable or has a fever, use pain relief only as generally recommended for their age and weight, and check with your GP, pharmacist, or child health nurse if you are unsure.
- **Keep sleep simple:** Follow tired signs, allow extra support to settle, and return to your usual plan once your baby seems back to normal.
- **Keep the sleep space safe:** Even if your baby is unsettled or extra sleepy, still use a flat, clear, back-to-sleep setup for every sleep.

## When to Seek Help
- Seek medical advice if symptoms are severe, last longer than a few days, or you are worried your baby is unwell for another reason.
- If your baby is under 3 months and has a fever, get urgent medical advice.
- Call 000 for breathing problems, a seizure, signs of a severe allergic reaction, or if your baby is very difficult to wake.
