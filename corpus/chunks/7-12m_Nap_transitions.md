# Nap Transitions: 7-12 Months

As your baby moves through 7 to 12 months, their sleep patterns continue to evolve, becoming more consolidated at night. During this period, most babies typically settle into a routine of two daytime naps. While the transition from three naps to two usually occurs earlier, this age band is about solidifying that two-nap schedule and preparing for future shifts.

Babies at this age generally need 11-14 hours of sleep over a 24-hour period. Their night-time sleep becomes the longest stretch, though waking once or twice for comfort or a feed is still common. Daytime naps usually consist of two sleeps, each lasting between 30 minutes to 2 hours. Wake windows continue to lengthen, ranging from 2.5 to 3.5 hours, or even up to 4 hours as they approach their first birthday.

Developmental milestones like crawling, pulling to stand, and separation anxiety can sometimes impact sleep, leading to temporary changes in settling or increased night waking. Remaining responsive and consistent with routines can help your baby navigate these phases.

## Key Points
- Babies typically have two daytime naps between 7-12 months.
- Total sleep is usually 11-14 hours in 24 hours, with longer night sleeps.
- Wake windows increase to 2.5-4 hours.
- Developmental leaps and separation anxiety can temporarily affect sleep.

## What to Try
- **Maintain a consistent bedtime routine:** A predictable sequence of calming activities like a bath, massage, stories, and cuddles helps signal sleep time.
- **Observe tired signs:** Continue to watch for cues like eye-rubbing, fussiness, or reduced interest in play, and respond by initiating the nap routine.
- **Encourage self-settling:** Place your baby in their cot drowsy but awake. If they wake during the night, pause to see if they can resettle themselves before offering comfort.
- **Use responsive settling techniques:** If your baby becomes distressed, offer gentle reassurance with your voice, patting, or a brief cuddle until they calm, then try to settle them back in their cot.
- **Adjust nap timing as needed:** If your baby is resisting a nap or seems overtired, consider slightly adjusting the timing of their naps or wake windows.

## When to Seek Help
- If your baby's sleep patterns are significantly disrupting their mood or development during the day.
- If you are finding it consistently difficult to settle your baby, or if night wakings are causing significant distress for you or your baby.
- If you suspect underlying health issues are affecting their sleep. Consult your child health nurse or GP for personalised advice.
