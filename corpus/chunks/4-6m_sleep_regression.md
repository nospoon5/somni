# The 4-Month Sleep Regression

Around 3 to 4 months of age, many babies experience a notable shift in their sleep patterns, often referred to as the "4-month sleep regression." This isn't a true regression in development, but rather a significant leap forward as your baby's sleep cycles mature and become more like an adult's. What was once a more disorganised sleep pattern now starts to develop a clearer day-night rhythm.

During this time, babies transition from two basic sleep states (active and quiet) to more distinct stages of light, deep, and dream sleep. Their sleep cycles also lengthen, meaning they may wake more frequently between these cycles and need help to resettle. This developmental change, combined with growing awareness of their surroundings, can lead to increased night waking and shorter naps. It's a normal and healthy part of their development, even if it feels challenging for parents.

## Key Points
- The 4-month sleep regression is a normal developmental stage, not a setback.
- Babies' sleep cycles mature, becoming more organised and adult-like.
- Increased awareness of their environment can lead to more frequent waking.
- Babies may need more support to connect sleep cycles and resettle.

## What to Try
- **Consistent Bedtime Routine:** Establish a calming and predictable bedtime routine to signal that it's time for sleep. This might include a bath, gentle massage, quiet stories, or lullabies.
- **Drowsy but Awake:** Aim to put your baby down in their cot when they are drowsy but still awake. This helps them learn to associate their sleep space with falling asleep independently.
- **Responsive Settling:** When your baby wakes or fusses, respond with comfort and reassurance. This could involve gentle patting, shushing, or briefly picking them up until they calm, then returning them to their cot. The goal is to provide support without creating new sleep associations.
- **Optimise Sleep Environment:** Ensure the room is dark and quiet, and maintain a comfortable temperature. White noise can be helpful to block out sudden disturbances.
- **Day-Night Differentiation:** Continue to emphasise the difference between day and night by keeping daytime bright and active, and nighttime dim and calm.

## When to Seek Help
- If your baby's sleep difficulties are causing significant stress or exhaustion for you, or if you have concerns about their overall health and development, please consult your child health nurse or GP.
- If responsive settling techniques aren't showing any improvement after a couple of weeks, a professional can help you explore other strategies.
