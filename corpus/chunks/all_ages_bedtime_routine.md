# Creating a Bedtime Routine

A consistent positive bedtime routine is a powerful cue for your baby's sleep. Doing the same predictable things in the same order every night tells their brain and body that sleep is coming, helping them wind down and settle faster.

Even young babies learn this pattern quickly. The routine doesn't need to be complex; it just needs to be predictable, repetitive, and happen roughly at the same point in their wake window.

## Key Points
- **The golden rule:** Keep it short but consistent—around 20-30 minutes is ideal for the whole process.
- **Atmosphere matters:** Dim the lights, speak softly, and reduce stimulating activities an hour before bed.
- **Feed early:** Try completing the last feed before they are overly tired, separating feeding from falling asleep by inserting a song or cuddle between the feed and the cot. This encourages independent settling.

## What to Try
- **Bath, Book, Bed**: This classic sequence works wonders. Warm water is relaxing, and the cooling of the body afterward triggers sleep hormones. Follow the bath with a quiet story or song in their dim bedroom, and then place them into the cot.
- **The "Wind-Down" transition**: 15 minutes before the routine starts, turn down the lights in the house, turn off screens in the baby's view, and move to quieter play to prevent that "second wind" of energy.
- **Keep it identical every night**: Whether it's a specific lullaby, zipping their sleep sack, a final cuddle, and the lights off, the goal is repetition so they expect sleep next.

## When to Seek Help
- A bedtime routine shouldn't be a battle. If your baby cries uncontrollably every time the routine begins or you feel overwhelming anxiety about bedtime, reach out to an early parenting service like Karitane or Tresillian for support.
