# Frequent Night Waking at 4-6 Months

Around 4 months, a massive change occurs in your baby's sleep. As their brain matures, their sleep cycles change drastically. What feels like a "regression" is often a permanent shift as they begin dropping into light, deep, and dream sleep just like adults. 

Because of these new cycles, babies wake fully every single 40–50 minutes overnight. If they don't know how to fall back to sleep the exact same way they first fell asleep, they call out for help—which is completely normal. 

## Key Points
- **The 4-Month Transition:** This sudden return to frequent waking usually begins around 3 to 4 months of age and can persist strongly until the 6-month mark. 
- **What Is A Sleep Association?** A sleep association is the condition under which they fall asleep (rocking, bouncing, feeding). When they wake lightly between cycles, they expect that same condition. 
- **The Role of Feeds:** It is typical for babies to need one or two feeds overnight at this age, but waking every 1-2 hours heavily suggests a sleep association. 

## What to Try
- **Observe the initial settling method:** Tonight, try placing your baby down slightly awake but calm, so they are fully aware they are in their cot before their eyes close. 
- **The Pause:** If they cry out at night, wait exactly one minute before jumping in. Often, babies cry out mid-cycle, but aren't fully awake. Giving them a second, or a brief comfort, gives them space to re-settle themselves. 
- **Avoid new habits:** Avoid introducing brand-new long-term settling habits right now—like pacing the halls at 3am. Try comforting them in the cot when possible. 

## When to Seek Help
- A 4-6 month old waking up multiple times to feed requires checkups. But if the night waking is making you severely anxious, extremely exhausted, or you feel you're struggling to cope with daily tasks, talk to your GP or child health nurse rather than weathering it alone.
