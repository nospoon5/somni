# Safe Sleeping Guidelines

Safe sleeping is the most important foundation for your baby's sleep. By following a few simple steps, you can significantly reduce the risk of sudden unexpected death in infancy (SUDI) and ensure your little one sleeps safely and soundly.

In Australia, we follow the Red Nose safe sleeping recommendations. These guidelines apply for every sleep, whether it's a short nap during the day or overnight.

## Key Points
- Always place your baby on their back to sleep, never on their tummy or side.
- Ensure your baby's face and head remain uncovered.
- Keep your baby in a smoke-free environment before and after birth.
- The safest place for your baby to sleep is in their own safe sleep space (cot or bassinet) in the same room as an adult caregiver.
- Breastfeeding your baby, if possible, is recommended.

## What to Try
- **Check the sleep space**: Ensure the mattress is firm, clean, well-fitting, and flat.
- **Clear the cot**: Remove all loose bedding, pillows, bumpers, soft toys, and sheepskins from the cot.
- **Dress appropriately**: Use a safe infant sleeping bag with fitted neck and armholes instead of blankets, so your baby stays warm without the risk of their face being covered.
- **Positioning**: If using blankets instead of a sleep sack, place baby with their feet at the bottom of the cot and tuck the blankets in securely across the chest.

## When to Seek Help
- If your baby is unwell, their safe sleeping needs do not change. However, if you are concerned about your baby's breathing or they have a fever, contact your GP immediately or call 000 in an emergency.
