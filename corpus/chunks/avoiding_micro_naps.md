# Avoiding Micro Naps

A micro nap is one of those tiny little sleeps that looks harmless on the surface, but can take the edge off sleep pressure and make the next nap much harder to land. Even a very short doze can reset a baby just enough that they are no longer properly ready for the nap you were aiming for.

That is why micro naps usually tell you something useful: the wake window was probably a little off, or your baby was not quite ready for sleep yet. The goal is not to force more sleep. The goal is to protect enough awake time so the body builds proper sleep drive and the next nap has a better chance of sticking.

If a micro nap happens, do not panic and do not treat the whole day as ruined. Just read it as a timing clue. In most cases, the best move is to push the next nap back a bit, let more awake time build, and then try again when your baby is properly sleepy. If you keep putting them down too early after a tiny snooze, you can get stuck in a frustrating cycle of short naps and low sleep pressure.

## Key Points
- A micro nap can lower sleep pressure without giving the body the full rest it actually needs.
- Short naps often mean the previous wake window was too short or the baby was not quite ready.
- The fix is usually a timing adjustment, not more frantic resettling.
- Pushing the next nap back a little can help the sleep drive build properly again.

## What to Try
- Treat a micro nap as information, not failure.
- Add a little more awake time before the next nap, then watch the cues closely.
- Keep the wake window calm and boring so your baby does not tip into overtiredness.
- If micro naps keep happening, look at the full day rhythm rather than just the one nap.

## When to Seek Help
- If your baby is regularly taking only tiny naps across the day and nights are also falling apart.
- If you are unsure whether the issue is undertiredness, overtiredness, or something medical.
- If sleep disruption is becoming too stressful for your family, get help from your GP or child health nurse.
