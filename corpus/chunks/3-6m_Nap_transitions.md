# Nap Transitions: 3-6 Months

Around 3 to 6 months of age, you might notice your baby's sleep patterns beginning to shift, often moving from many short, irregular naps towards a more predictable schedule of two or three daytime sleeps. This is a common nap transition, as babies start to consolidate their sleep and their wake windows naturally extend.

At this age, babies typically sleep between 12-15 hours over a 24-hour period. While they still need frequent night feeds, their longest stretch of sleep often starts to occur at night, sometimes reaching 4-6 hours by 6 months. During the day, they might begin to settle into a pattern of 2-3 naps, each lasting up to 2 hours. The third, late-afternoon nap often becomes shorter or optional as they approach 6 months.

Your baby's wake windows, the time they can comfortably stay awake between sleeps, will also increase from around 1.5-2 hours at 3-4 months to 2-3 hours by 5-6 months. Observing these cues and adapting your routine can help ease this transition.

## Key Points
- Babies typically transition from multiple naps to 2-3 daytime naps between 3-6 months.
- The third, late-afternoon nap may become shorter or optional.
- Total sleep is usually 12-15 hours in 24 hours, with longer night stretches emerging.
- Wake windows increase from 1.5-2 hours to 2-3 hours.

## What to Try
- **Watch for tired signs:** Look for yawning, eye-rubbing, fussiness, or staring into space. Responding promptly helps prevent overtiredness.
- **Establish a flexible "feed, play, sleep" pattern:** This helps your baby anticipate what comes next, but remain responsive to their individual hunger and tired cues.
- **Introduce a calming bedtime routine:** A consistent sequence of activities like a bath, gentle massage, pyjamas, quiet stories, and cuddles can signal it's time for sleep.
- **Put your baby down drowsy but awake:** This encourages them to learn to settle themselves in their cot, which can help them resettle independently if they wake between sleep cycles.
- **Use responsive settling:** If your baby fusses or cries, offer comfort with gentle words, patting, or rocking. If very distressed, pick them up to calm them before trying to settle them back in their cot.

## When to Seek Help
- If your baby is consistently very difficult to settle, even with responsive techniques.
- If you are concerned about your baby's overall sleep duration or mood during wake periods.
- If you are feeling overwhelmed or struggling with your baby's sleep patterns. Your child and family health nurse or GP can offer tailored advice and support.
